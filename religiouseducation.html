RELIGIOUS EDUCATION

Adult Failth Formation Program

Adult Faith Formation encompasses programs designed for adult learning of all ages, such as workshops and instruction on various topics, evenings or days of reflection, small sharing groups, Scripture study groups and retreat opportunities. Its focus includes family activities such as family meals at the parish, family prayer experiences, pilgrimages and family outings. For those adults who are coming into the faith, the R.C.I.A. (Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults) program is available. The "Landings" program is offered for those who have been away from the church or the sacraments for any length of time and are considering a return. (See further information on these two programs in this directory.)

Articles of Faith


Our next Year of Faith meeting will be Sunday, August 4th, at 7:00 PM in the School Library. We will be studying the 10th Article of Faith in the Creed, "I confess one Baptism for the forgiveness of sins." We will study this by viewing and discussing Lesson 5 of the DVD Series Catholicism, by Father Robert Barron, "The Indispensible Men : Peter, Paul, and the Missionary Adventure." What we will learn is that the Lord gave the Apostles, represented by Peter and Paul, the power to forgive Sins. This commission is justified in the readings of both the Old and New Testaments. The Home Study Guide for the ninth Article of Faith will be distributed in a future Sunday Bulletin and is available from the parish website: www.saintphilipnerichurch.org

Parish Religious Education/CCD

Directed by Monsignor Vance, our parish Religious Education Program includes CCD, sacramental preparation for children, family retreats for those who are preparing to receive the sacraments of Penance and Confirmation, evenings or days of reflection, workshops, yearly mission and instruction on our faith by parish staff or assigned speakers. For those who are coming into the faith, we have the R.C.I.A. program (Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults) under the direction of Msgr. Vance. The responsibility of assisting in the direction of Adult Education Program is Ms. Jeanette Paul, D.R.E. (Director of Religious Education).
